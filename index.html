I am a doctoral researcher at the Center for Machine Vision and Signal Analysis (CMVS), Faculty of Information Technology and Electrical Engineering, University of Oulu, Finland. My research focuses on applying artificial intelligence and deep learning to real-world healthcare challenges — particularly in medical image analysis, disease detection, and large language models for clinical decision support.

My work spans a broad spectrum of healthcare AI: from X-ray-based detection of cardiopulmonary conditions (cardiomegaly, pneumonia, atelectasis) to smart IoT-integrated diagnostics for infectious diseases such as malaria and cancer screening. I leverage state-of-the-art deep learning architectures — CNNs, DenseNet-169, ResNet-50, Inception, and Involutional Neural Networks — combined with advanced image processing techniques to build clinically relevant AI systems.

Before joining the University of Oulu, I spent six years as a university lecturer and educational quality assurance coordinator in Ethiopia, contributing to teaching and research in computer science and information technology. I am also an active peer reviewer, having evaluated 60+ manuscripts across 15+ international journals, conferences, and workshops including NeurIPS events.

Research

Bridging artificial intelligence and real-world healthcare through medical image analysis, deep learning, and smart diagnostic systems.

Medical Image Diagnostics

Deep learning detection and classification of diseases from chest X-rays: cardiomegaly (99.8% accuracy via ResNet-50), pneumonia (YOLOv3 + SVM pipeline), atelectasis (CNNs with Anisotropic Diffusion filtering), and GI diseases from endoscopy videos using two-phase transfer learning.

X-ray / Endoscopy ResNet / DenseNet Transfer Learning

Smart IoT Healthcare Diagnostics

AI-powered disease monitoring systems integrating wearable sensors, edge computing, and cloud ML models. Malaria classification (SVM/ANN/KNN with 96% test accuracy), breast cancer screening with Involutional Neural Networks, and real-time predictive analytics for clinical deployment.

IoT + ML Edge Computing Wearables

LLMs & NLP for Healthcare

Applying large language models in clinical settings: automating administrative data processing, reducing documentation workload, and generating real-time predictive analytics to support medical decision-making and keep practitioners informed of patient trends and medical literature.

LLMs Clinical NLP Healthcare Automation
